Your Laptop Isn't a Bop-It

Presented by: Geoff Cureton

Thursday, January 8 2026, 6:30 PM
Madison Public Library, 201 W Mifflin St, Room 302 [Map]
RSVP on Meetup

For a lot of office jobs, but for software developers in particular, the keyboard is the primary means by which you perform your tasks and get through the day. But other than raw typing speed, scant consideration is generally given to the benefits of a keyboard-centric workflow. In this presentation I will describe my journey from a Win3.1 using, mouse-dependent terrible student developer, to a mechanical keyboard typing, Vim using, "Arch BTW!" huffing, mid professional developer!

Keyboards, text editors, terminal multiplexers, desktop environments, tiling window managers, I'll (try to) cover them all! Your laptop isn't a Bop-It™, stop using it like one!

Geoff Cureton is a physicist, data engineer and python developer at the Cooperative Institute for Meteorological Satellite Studies at the University of Wisconsin-Madison.
